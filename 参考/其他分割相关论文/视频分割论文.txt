(1)Coarse-to-Fine Video Instance Segmentation With Factorized Conditional Appearance Flows
- **Task**: This paper focuses on video instance segmentation (VIS), which involves automatically tracking, segmenting, and classifying all instances in a video sequence. It also demonstrates the method's generalizability on the unsupervised video object segmentation (UVOS) task 🔶1-17🔶.

- **Proposed Method**: A novel generative model with hierarchical structural embedding learning is introduced. It adopts a coarse-to-fine approach: first coarsely locating instance pixels using Gaussian distribution, then building a mixing distribution by fusing hierarchical appearance embedding information. A factorization condition normalized flow (factorized conditional appearance flows) is utilized to estimate distribution parameters for improved segmentation. The whole pipeline is end-to-end trainable, without needing separate sub-networks for detection and association . Three key contributions are: exploiting a hierarchical pipeline from a generative model perspective, enabling stable training with a single network, and being the first to employ factorized conditional normalizing flows in VIS for instance appearance distribution estimation .

(2)Deep Common Feature Mining for Efficient Video Semantic Segmentation
The paper focuses on video semantic segmentation, aiming to address challenges like redundant computation and unreliable feature propagation while balancing accuracy, inference efficiency, and temporal consistency .

The proposed method is Deep Common Feature Mining (DCFM). It decomposes features into common and independent representations: the common one from key-frames provides essential high-level information reusable by neighboring non-key frames without propagation, and the independent one from each frame captures frame-specific clues . A symmetric training strategy tailored for sparsely annotated data helps the backbone learn robust high-level common information, and a self-supervised loss reinforces intra-class feature similarity and temporal consistency . Additionally, a lightweight fusion module integrates multi-level features for comprehensive representation and efficiency .

(3)DeformFlowNet: A deformable transformer with optical flow constraint for interventricular septum segmentation in echocardiography videos
**Task**: This study focuses on the automatic segmentation of the interventricular septum in transthoracic echocardiography (TTE) videos, aiming to provide a reliable foundation for subsequent left ventricular hypertrophy (LVH) etiological diagnosis .

**Proposed method and innovations**: 
1. A hybrid 3D network named DeformFlowNet is proposed, which combines U-shaped attention networks and optical flow constraints for the segmentation task .
2. A deformable transformer (DTFormer) block is added to the skip connection between the U-shaped network’s encoder and decoder, capturing long-range dependencies of multi-level encoded feature maps while balancing computational and spatial complexity .
3. An optical flow consistency constraint module is introduced after the decoder to establish long-range relationships between TTE video frames and refine the mask images of intermediate frames .
4. A joint optimization learning supervision design is adopted, combining U-shaped attention network loss and optical flow loss through weighted machine learning to guide better model training .

(5)Distortion Map-Guided Feature Rectification for Efficient Video Semantic Segmentation
The task of this paper is video semantic segmentation, which aims to assign dense class labels to each frame in a video while meeting the efficiency requirements of real-time applications .

The proposed method is a distortion map-guided feature rectification approach. It targets feature updating and correction on distorted regions with unreliable optical flow. A lightweight correction network (CoNet) is designed to extract updated features for distorted regions. A distortion map, generated in a coarse-to-fine manner by combining a coarse distortion map (detecting occlusions and rough moving boundaries from label inconsistency) and an edge map (locating boundaries and thin objects from a lightweight edge & thin object perceiving module), serves as weighted attention to guide feature rectification by aggregating warped features and updated features. Additionally, an auxiliary edge-semantics loss is proposed to implement class-specific supervision on distorted regions. The network is trained end-to-end and is highly modular .

(6)Dual Correlation Network for Efficient Video Semantic Segmentation
The task of this paper is video semantic segmentation, addressing challenges like large data volume and strong inter-frame redundancy in video data.

The proposed method is a dual local and global correlation network, which includes three innovative modules. Firstly, a local attention based module that measures correlation and achieves feature aggregation in a local region between key frame and non-key frame in a non-parametric way, enhancing efficiency without relying on extra network modules. Secondly, a consistent constraint module that considers long-range correlation among pixels from a global view to promote intra-frame semantic consistency of non-key frames, improving accuracy without increasing inference load, inspired by knowledge distillation and using pair-wise similarity as knowledge. Thirdly, a key frame decision module that adaptively selects key frames based on feature transferring ability, defined by potential prediction error (PPE) to measure propagating ability, differing from fixed interval selection.

(7)DVIS: Decoupled Video Instance Segmentation Framework
- **Task**: The paper focuses on video instance segmentation (VIS), a critical computer vision task involving identifying, segmenting, and tracking all interested instances in a video simultaneously, with applications in autonomous driving and video editing. It also extends to video panoptic segmentation (VPS).

- **Proposed Method**: The paper proposes a decoupled VIS framework called DVIS, which divides VIS into three independent sub-tasks: segmentation, tracking, and refinement. 
  - A referring tracker with a core Referring Cross Attention (RCA) module is designed to model inter-frame association as a referring denoising task, encouraging interaction between instance representations of adjacent frames while avoiding information mixing.
  - A temporal refiner is introduced, which uses 1D convolution and self-attention to integrate temporal information and cross-attention to correct instance representations, effectively utilizing information from the entire video.
  - The decoupling strategy allows separate training of components, reducing computational costs.

(8)DVIS++: Improved Decoupled Framework for Universal Video Segmentation
**Task:**
The paper introduces a unified framework for universal video segmentation, which is designed to handle multiple video segmentation tasks simultaneously, including video instance segmentation (VIS), video semantic segmentation (VSS), and video panoptic segmentation (VPS)[cite: 3, 21]. The framework is also extended to perform open-vocabulary universal video segmentation[cite: 10].

**Proposed Method & Innovations:**
The core innovation is a decoupled framework that breaks down the complex video segmentation problem into three simpler, cascaded sub-tasks: segmentation, tracking, and refinement[cite: 4, 60]. To address these sub-tasks, the paper proposes:
* A novel **Referring Tracker**, which is a learnable module that models the frame-by-frame object association as a denoising or reconstruction process[cite: 6, 67].
* A novel **Temporal Refiner**, which models the spatio-temporal representations of objects using the pre-aligned features generated by the tracker[cite: 6, 7].
* An improved version named **DVIS++**, which enhances the tracking capability by incorporating a **denoising training strategy** to simulate challenging cases and uses **contrastive learning** to obtain more discriminative object representations.

(9)Efficient Video Object Segmentation via Modulated Cross-Attention Memory
- **Task**: The paper focuses on semi-supervised video object segmentation (VOS), aiming to address the challenges of increased GPU memory demands and reduced speed faced by transformer-based approaches when processing long videos, while maintaining segmentation accuracy .

- **Proposed Method**: The proposed method is MAVOS, which introduces an optimized and dynamic long-term modulated cross-attention (MCA) memory. This MCA effectively encodes both local and global features at various levels of granularity, models temporal smoothness without requiring frequent memory expansion, and allows propagation of target information based on temporal changes of past frames. The architecture of MAVOS includes the Efficient Long Short-Term Transformer (E-LSTT) block, which consists of short-term propagation, long-term propagation (based on MCA memory), and self-propagation .

(10)End-to-End Video Semantic Segmentation in Adverse Weather using Fusion Blocks and Temporal-Spatial Teacher-Student Learning
**Task**: This paper focuses on end-to-end video semantic segmentation in adverse weather conditions, addressing the issue that current methods suffer from erroneous predictions due to degraded video frames and unreliable optical flows under such conditions.

**Proposed Method**: 
- It introduces the first end-to-end, optical-flow-free, domain-adaptive video semantic segmentation method.
- A fusion block is designed to merge feature-level information from adjacent frames by matching and merging relevant pixels, enabling the model to exploit temporal information without relying on optical flows.
- A temporal-spatial teacher-student learning approach is developed, involving a temporal teacher (exploring temporal information from adjacent frames) and a spatial teacher (harnessing spatial information from the current frame) to train the fusion block and enhance pseudo-label quality.
- Temporal weather degradation augmentation is applied to consecutive frames, with correlated augmentations at corresponding locations and gradual intensity changes to simulate adverse weather characteristics.

(11)First-frame Supervised Video Polyp Segmentation via Propagative and Semantic Dual-teacher Network
- **Task**: The paper focuses on First-Frame Supervised Video Polyp Segmentation (FSVPS), which aims to perform automatic video polyp segmentation with only the first frame annotated per polyp video, regardless of the video's length, to reduce the high cost of frame-by-frame annotations .

- **Proposed Method**: A novel Propagative and Semantic Dual-Teacher Network (PSDNet) is proposed. It adopts a teacher-student framework with two distinct teachers: a propagative teacher (an off-the-shelf universal video object segmentation model, XMem, to propagate the first-frame annotation to subsequent frames as pseudo labels) and a semantic teacher (an exponential moving average of the student model, providing more stable and time-invariant pseudo labels). Additionally, a back-propagation scoring method is introduced to assess the quality of the pseudo labels from both teachers (by propagating pseudo labels back to the first frame and measuring consistency with the first-frame annotation) and merge them to guide the student model .

(12)Iterative feedback-based models for image and video polyp segmentation
The task of this paper is to address the challenge of accurate segmentation of polyps in colonoscopy videos, which is crucial for automated colorectal cancer diagnosis, considering issues like frequent camera shake and frame defocusing in endoscopic imaging .

The proposed method is FlowICBNet, which extends the Iterative Feedback Unit (IFU) from image polyp segmentation to video polyp segmentation. It leverages the continuity of video content by using segmentation results of previous frames as initialization masks for iterative refinement via IFU. Two key modules are introduced: Reference Frame Selection (RFS) that considers both the quality of candidate frame predictions and their distance from the current frame to select reliable reference frames, and Flow Guided Warping (FGW) that uses optical flow to warp the reference frame's prediction to generate the initial mask for the current frame .

(13)Learning Deep Spatio-Temporal Dependence for Semantic Video Segmentation
**Task**: The paper focuses on semantic video segmentation, which aims to assign semantic labels to every voxel (pixels from a spatio-temporal viewpoint) in a video, considering the complex spatio-temporal dependencies within the video .

**Proposed Method and Innovations**: 
The paper proposes a novel deep convolutional network architecture called deep spatio-temporal fully convolutional networks (DST-FCN). It leverages both spatial and temporal dependencies through a two-stream network:
- A 2D FCN followed by convolutional long short-term memory (ConvLSTM) on the pixel level to explore long-term temporal dependence .
- A 3D FCN on the voxel level to enable voxel-level prediction .
The entire architecture is trained end-to-end, and the outputs of the two streams are combined via linear fusion .

(14)Learning Local and Global Temporal Contexts for Video Semantic Segmentation
**Task**: This paper focuses on video semantic segmentation (VSS), aiming to leverage temporal contextual information to improve segmentation performance. It addresses the challenge of effectively utilizing both local and global temporal contexts, which are crucial for understanding dynamic video content.

**Proposed Methods**:
1. **Coarse-to-Fine Feature Mining (CFFM)**: Designed to learn a unified representation of local temporal contexts (LTC), which include static and motional contexts from neighboring frames. It consists of two parts:
   - **Coarse-to-Fine Feature Assembling (CFFA)**: Abstracts static and motional contexts by processing features from neighboring frames with varying receptive fields and pooling granularity based on their distance from the target frame.
   - **Cross-frame Feature Mining (CFM)**: A non-self attention mechanism that mines useful information from neighboring frames to enhance target frame features, with queries from the target frame and keys/values from neighboring frames.

2. **CFFM++**: An extension of CFFM that further incorporates global temporal contexts (GTC) from the entire video. It uniformly samples frames from the video, extracts global contextual prototypes via k-means clustering, and uses CFM to mine information from these prototypes to refine target features.

(15)LGRNet: Local-Global Reciprocal Network for Uterine Fibroid Segmentation in Ultrasound Videos(时序信息传播用的是注意力)
**Task**: This paper focuses on uterine fibroid segmentation in ultrasound videos. It collects and annotates the first ultrasound video dataset (UFUV) for this task, which contains 100 videos with per-frame expert annotations .

(16)LiVOS: Light Video Object Segmentation with Gated Linear Matching
**Task**: The paper focuses on semi-supervised video object segmentation (VOS), where the segmentation of the first frame is provided, and the model needs to propagate this segmentation across subsequent frames .

**Proposed Method**: To address the memory limitations of space-time memory (STM) networks caused by the quadratic complexity of softmax matching, the paper proposes LiVOS, a lightweight memory network. It employs linear matching via linear attention, reformulating memory matching into a recurrent process that reduces the quadratic attention matrix to a constant-size, spatiotemporal-agnostic 2D state. Additionally, gated linear matching is introduced, where a data-dependent gate matrix is multiplied with the state matrix to control information retention or discarding .

(18)MemSAM: Taming Segment Anything Model for Echocardiography Video Segmentation
**Task**: The paper focuses on echocardiography video segmentation, aiming to address long-standing challenges in this field, including massive speckle noise and artifacts, extremely ambiguous boundaries, and large variations of targeting objects across frames, especially with limited and sparse annotations .

**Proposed Method**: The proposed model is MemSAM, which adapts the Segment Anything Model (SAM) to medical videos through a temporal-aware and noise-resilient prompting scheme. (19)RMem: Restricted Memory Banks Improve Video Object Segmentation
**Task**: The paper focuses on video object segmentation (VOS), particularly addressing challenging scenarios such as significant object state changes (e.g., on the VOST dataset) and long-duration videos (e.g., on the Long Videos dataset) .

**Proposed Method**: The study introduces "RMem" (Restricted Memory Banks), which revisits the strategy of restricting memory bank sizes instead of expanding them. The key innovations include:
1. A "memory deciphering" analysis revealing that expanding memory banks increases decoding difficulty due to redundant information, hindering VOS modules' ability to extract relevant features .
2. A restricted memory bank with a fixed number of frames, balanced for frame relevance and freshness using a strategy inspired by the upper confidence bound (UCB) algorithm from multi-arm bandit problems .
3. Introduction of temporal positional embedding to enhance spatio-temporal reasoning, enabled by reduced training-inference discrepancy in memory lengths due to the restricted memory bank .

(20)SALI: Short-term Alignment and Long-term Interaction Network for Colonoscopy Video Polyp Segmentation
The task of this paper is to address the problem of automatic polyp segmentation in colonoscopy videos, which is crucial for providing timely prompts and richer information for colorectal cancer diagnosis .

The paper proposes a novel network called SALI (Short-term Alignment and Long-term Interaction Network). Its innovations lie in two modules: the Short-term Alignment Module (SAM) and the Long-term Interaction Module (LIM). SAM aligns features of adjacent frames using deformable convolution to mitigate spatial variations caused by fast camera movement and close-up observation, then aggregates these aligned features to construct stable short-term representations. LIM establishes a memory bank to store historical polyp representations and uses a masked-attention block to explore retrospective relations, enabling the reconstruction of more reliable polyp features for current segmentation, especially in the presence of low-quality frames .

(21)Semantic Video CNNs through Representation Warping
**Task**: The paper focuses on converting CNN models for semantic segmentation of static images into CNNs suitable for video data, aiming to develop fast and accurate video semantic segmentation methods that utilize temporal coherence in video frames while reusing strong single-image segmentation CNNs .

**Proposed method**: The key innovation is the NetWarp module, which warps intermediate CNN representations of the previous frame to corresponding representations of the current frame using optical flow between adjacent frames. It involves three steps: flow transformation (via a small CNN called FlowCNN to optimize optical flow), representation warping (using bilinear interpolation based on transformed flow), and combination of representations (linear combination of warped previous frame representations and current frame representations). Multiple NetWarp modules can be used at different layers of the CNN hierarchy, is fully differentiable for end-to-end training, and enables online processing of video frames with minimal extra computational cost .
